# Dink Card Compliance Positioning

Last updated: May 31, 2026

## Business Model

Dink Card is a digital service platform that helps verified customers request virtual card-related services through authorized third-party providers.

## What Dink Card Is

- A digital service facilitator/reseller platform.
- A platform for verified users to add service credit and request supported virtual card-related services.
- A support, review, and account-management layer for Dink Card customers.

## What Dink Card Is Not

- Dink Card is not a bank.
- Dink Card is not a card network.
- Dink Card is not a payment institution or payment gateway.
- Dink Card is not a wallet provider, money transfer service, or remittance provider.
- Dink Card is not a crypto exchange.
- Dink Card is not a direct card issuer.

## Third-Party Provider Role

Virtual card approval, activation, availability, limits, restrictions, fees, refunds, and merchant acceptance may depend on authorized third-party providers, provider rules, risk review, compliance checks, merchant rules, network rules, and technical availability.

## Service Credit Explanation

Any balance or credit shown inside Dink Card is service credit only. It may only be used by the verified account owner to request Dink Card services. It is not a bank deposit, e-money account, stored-value wallet, payment account, withdrawable balance, or transferable financial balance.

## No P2P Policy

Dink Card does not support peer-to-peer transfers, user-to-user balance transfers, balance resale, third-party payments, cash-out, crypto trading, money exchange, or remittance activity. The user-to-user transfer endpoints are blocked and the user-facing send money action is hidden.

## Admin Review Workflow

Admins review deposits, KYC submissions, card requests, support tickets, suspicious activity, rejected requests, refunds, and manual balance actions. Sensitive decisions should include a reason and are recorded in audit logs.

## Record Keeping Workflow

Dink Card stores account records, service credit records, transaction records, KYC submissions, payment proofs, card request records, provider responses, support records, notifications, and admin audit logs for operational, fraud-prevention, dispute, compliance, and security purposes.
